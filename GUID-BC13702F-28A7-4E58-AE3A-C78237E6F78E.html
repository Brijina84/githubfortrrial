Product Categories and Products

In the Strategy Management Workstation, you can organize related products into product categories. Access product categories through Categories and Products from the Item Types tab.
Categories are logical groups that suit your business needs. The following example lists some logical groups:
  • Lines of business, such as credit cards, auto loans, consumer loans, or small business loans
  • Events, such as pricing
  • Families of lending products, such as premier credit cards, alumni credit cards, or student credit cards
  • Specific scoring populations, such as students, that are subsets of your portfolio and represent a unique risk or require a unique treatment.
A product category can have many products under it. For example, if an organization creates a category called "General Unsecured Loan Pricing Event", then it can create pricing products such as:
  • Up to $15,000
  • Between $15,000 and $50,000
  • Between $50,000 and $100,000
  • Between $100,000 and $250,000
  • For VIPs with over $250,000 in accounts

In these pricing products, the decision-making process of determining rates, fees, credit limits, and other loan terms are executed.

A product can be any loan, credit card, or other service products provided by an institution. For each product, you assign a product strategy (referencing one or more decision flows) or a default decision flow, or both. When a decision flow executes, its steps invoke rulesets, decision tables, decision trees, data method sequences, and score models that implement your policies.

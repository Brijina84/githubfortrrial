Score Model Calculations

The final score of a score model represents an assessment, opinion, or prediction about the customer or prospect. The calculation of a final score is a multi-step process during which a raw score is adjusted and aligned and intermediate scores (adjusted score and aligned score) are produced.

The steps differ based on the model calculation type used: additive or logistic regression. Select either Additive or Logistic Regression in the Score Model Properties page when you define a score model.

Covered Topics
  • Additive Calculations
    In additive calculations, the raw score of the model is the value of the adjusted score that is used in the process for determining the final score.
  • Logistic Regression Calculations
    In logistic regression calculations, the adjusted score of the model is obtained by applying an adjustment amount and a regression formula to the raw score. The adjusted score is then used in the process for determining the final score.
  • Log Odds Calculations
    The log odds ratio represents the probability or likelihood of the observed data over the expected value.

Approving or Declining Applications

When an application requires a manual review and decision by an analyst or manager, it is placed in a queue your administrator may have defined for pending reviews where you can access and review all gathered data and make a decision, or request additional data. With synchronous consumer applications, there is no queue and you can open the application directly.
  1. To access an application that is in a review queue, click the queue that your administrator may have created and defined for pending review status in the My Queues pane on the Home page.
    Note When you are working on the priority queue, the applications are displayed in the sequence that is determined by your administrator.
  2. For synchronous consumer credit applications, directly open the application.
  3. Review the information presented in the Application Overview, Details of <Applicant Name>, Decision Scores, and Credit Offer Details panes.
  4. To review additional information about the application, click on any link that appears in the Application Information navigation pane.

    The options that appear vary based on your permissions, and may include any of the following:

    • Application Overview
    • Details of <Applicant Name>
    • Decision Scores
    • Credit Offer Details
    • Decision
  5. In the Credit Offer Details pane, use Evaluate to evaluate a CRA purchase and score for an applicant.
    If the application needs any editing, use Edit this application from the side menu.
  6. In the Decision pane, select a decision from the options presented.
    Decision options may include the following items:
    • Approve
    • Decline
    • Exit Without Saving
    • Withdraw Application
    • and open next case
    Note Your view depends on what the administrators may have configured for you. The application is processed based on the decision option you have selected.
  7. In the Score Override reason field, select a reason for overriding the original requested offer.
    This field becomes required only in one of the following scenarios:
    • When the recommended score decision is Approve and you are declining the application.
    • When the recommended score decision is Decline and you are approving the application.
    In all other cases, the Score Override reason is non-mandatory.
  8. Click Approve.
    Alternately, click Decline if you must decline the credit application. You can also use the Exit Without Saving option.
  9. (Optional) Select the and open next case check box to access the next case in the queue.
  10. (Optional) To withdraw the application from the system, click Withdraw Application.

Call Activities

Call activities are generic smallest sub-flows in a process. Call activities created from events and tasks and do not contain any sub-process. A sub-process or process can have multiple call-activites. Seven types of call activities are available.

Call activities are of the following types:

REST Service

Make a REST call from your process or sub-process. You must configure the necessary settings and parameters to invoke the external REST services

SOAP Service

Make a SOAP service from your process or sub-process. You must configure the necessary settings and parameters to invoke the external SOAP services.

Service Task

Data Access Call

Rule Execution Service

A rule execution call is a call made to a decision service. At some point in you workflow, when you can want some decisioning, you can call a rule that you have authored in the Business Strategies workbench for that purpose. For example, a business rule can be if the applicant is old enough to qualify for a loan or does the applicant have the minimum income required. Based on the business logic, the decision rule is called, executed, and a result or decision is obtained.

The continue on errors is a flag that determines whether the workflow will stop execution in an error occurs. If selected or set to true, the execution stops and returns an error flag. If the checkbox is not selected or set to false, the workflow will log the exception; the execution does not stop and proceeds to the next item or step in the workflow.

  • DM Function. The Business Strategies Function that will provide the decisioning. All the functions that you have authored are available in the drop-down list. Mandatory parameter.

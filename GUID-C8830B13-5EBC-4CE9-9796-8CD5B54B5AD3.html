Business Processes Workbench

The Business Processes Workbench is a tool that lets you create, view, and modify a business process or a worflow. Use the business process canvas and the set of tools and elements to create a business process. You can create sub-processes and connect them to form a workflow that reflect the business processes used to decision a credit application. You can create multiple workflows. At any time, you can add or modify the workflow components to reflect changes in the processing.

You can include human activities to handle those events that cannot be automated, where users are requried to take the necessary action.

Covered Topics
  • Business Processes Designer
    The Business Processes workbench provides a designer for creating a business process or a workflow.
  • Events
    An event is the smallest and most basic element in a business process or workflow. There are seven generic predefined events that you can use to in a workflow or a subflow. Start and stop are two events that must be present in a workflow or a subflow.
  • Gateways
    Gateways control the flow of execution in a business process. Use a gateway to control the divergence of convergence of multiple sequence flows.
  • Tasks
    Tasks are activities in a business process or workflow. A task is the actual work being done in a workflow or business process.
  • Call Activities
    Call activities are generic smallest sub-flows in a process. Call activities created from events and tasks and do not contain any sub-process. A sub-process or process can have multiple call-activites. Seven types of call activities are available.
  • Sub-processes
    A sub-process is a process within a parent process. You can group a set of steps that are specific to a scenario. You construct a sub-process with the components, such as events, gateways, call-activities, and so on. You can construct a workflow using multiple such sub-processes. In addition, you can use the same sub-process in multiple business processes or workflows.

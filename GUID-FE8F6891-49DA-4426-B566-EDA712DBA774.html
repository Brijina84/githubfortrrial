Sub-processes

A sub-process is a process within a parent process. You can group a set of steps that are specific to a scenario. You construct a sub-process with the components, such as events, gateways, call-activities, and so on. You can construct a workflow using multiple such sub-processes. In addition, you can use the same sub-process in multiple business processes or workflows.

You can consider a sub-process as a building block of business process. Instead of creating a entire workflow in a single shot mode, you can create sub-process and then use them to construct a business process or workflow.

While a sub-process inherits the variables from the parent process where it is used, iIt can have local variables, which are visible only within that sub-process.

Covered Topics
  • Sub-process - Make Decision
    The Make Decision sub-process is a pre-configured out-of-the-box sub-process available for use in a business process. The sub-process makes a call to the pre-configured decision service to get a decision on the application. You must use this sub-process after the usual eligibility checks have been performed and the required data is obtained from the credit bureaus. This information is then sent to the decision service. The decision service returns two outputs, decision and status. The output decision can have a value such as, review, decline, recommend decline, investigate, recommend approve, approve, or no decision. The output status indicates whether the call was successful or whether an error occurred. Apart from these two outputs, the sub-process sends the request and response IDs.
  • Sub-process - Customer Eligibility Check
    The Customer Eligibility Check sub-process is a pre-configured out-of-the-box sub-process available for use in a business process. The sub-process checks the eligibilty of the applicant against four parameters, gross monthly income, net monthly income, age, and postal code. The sub-process makes a call to the pre-configured decision service to perform the eligibility check. The decision service returns two outputs, decision and status. The output decision can have a value such as, review, decline, or investigate. The output status indicates whether the call was successful or whether an error occurred.
  • Sub-process - Experian US Consumer
    The Experian US Consumer sub-process is a pre-configured out-of-the-box sub-process available for use in a business process. The sub-process sends a request to the Experian US Consumer bureau along with the necessary information required to connect to the bureau. This sub-process is available to you if the bureau has been configured for you during the on-boarding process. The Experian US Consumer configuration is done so that all the connection parameters are specified.
  • Sub-process - Approve Application
    The Approve Application sub-process is a pre-configured out-of-the-box sub-process available for use in a business process. The sub-process moves the application to approved with the reason text (the reason for approving the application). When the processing workflow calls the decision service for a decision, the decision service returns the decisions and reasons to the client application and the information is saved in the application BOM.
  • Sub-process - Decline Application
    The Decline Application sub-process is a pre-configured out-of-the-box sub-process available for use in a business process. The sub-process declines the application with the reason text (the reason for declining the application). When the processing workflow calls the decision service for a decision, the decision service returns the decisions and reasons to the client application and the information is saved in the application BOM.

Score Models

A score model is a mathematical formula used to predict the likely behavior of a customer or prospect. A score model uses information currently known about the customer or prospect to produce a predictive value. For example, you can create a score model to predict the likelihood that a borrower will default on a loan, or predict how likely it is that a person will accept a particular marketing offer.

Score models can be deployed using the Score Model implementation type and can be executed directly from a decision flow step or from a scoring scenario within a decision tree action node. The Score Models list page is used to access existing score models or add new ones. The Score Model Properties page is used to define or modify score models and their properties.

Covered Topics
  • Score Model Characteristics
    Known facts (such as income or months at current address) are used as input values by the score model, and are referred to as characteristics. A characteristicâ€™s data type can be numeric (integer or real) or string.
  • Score Model Execution
    In a score model, the values you enter in the bin columns are used to generate underlying rule conditions.
  • Score Model Calculations
    The final score of a score model represents an assessment, opinion, or prediction about the customer or prospect. The calculation of a final score is a multi-step process during which a raw score is adjusted and aligned and intermediate scores (adjusted score and aligned score) are produced.
  • Reason Codes
    You can create three types of score models using the Strategy Management Workstation.
  • Validating Score Models
    The Decision Module provides two ways to verify that the values entered in the score model are valid: characteristic-level validation and model-level validation.
  • Calling Score Models
    Score Models are decision flow implementation types and can be called from either a decision flow step or from a scoring scenario used in a decision tree.
  • Score Model Reports
    Score models generate characteristic data for reporting.
  • Managing Score Models
    Use the options on the list and properties pages for score models to create, copy, and delete score models, and define characteristics and bins.

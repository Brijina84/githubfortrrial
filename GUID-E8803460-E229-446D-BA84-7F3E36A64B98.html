Managing Product Strategies

A product strategy is a specialized decision tree that determines which decision flow executes for a product. The Decision Module derives a decision using a product strategy that is based on a organizations business rules and policies.

Access Product Strategies from the Item Types tab. A product strategy is configured in a Product Strategy Properties page.

Assign a product strategy to one or more products by selecting it in the product’s properties page. A product must be assigned a default decision flow or a product strategy that references at least one decision flow.

A product strategy has condition nodes and action nodes. The condition nodes specify the decision processing logic. The action nodes specify the decision flows that may be run. A product strategy can contain just one decision flow or multiple decision flows to be evaluated, with one decision flow selected at the end of the evaluation process. If a product does not have an assigned product strategy or if the product strategy does not specify a decision flow, the product’s default decision flow is used.

Note If you do not define a default decision flow and the product strategy does not return one for a specific request, the Decision Module will not execute any decision flow for that request.

Product strategies use standard Decision Module input mappings. This allows you to configure the product strategy to use any field from the object model to determine which decision flow to select.
